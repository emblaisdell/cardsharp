# The Table-less Card Game

The Table-less Card Game is a standard deck game that can be played standing up with no table; all cards are kept in players' physical hands.

The deck starts evenly distributed randomly among players.

One designated "counter" player progressively counts off 1,2,3,...  The count resets at 1 each round.

On the count of n, each player looks (privately) at the top n cards of their deck.

At any point, any player can call out the value (not suit) of a card they see (one of the top n).  The player with the highest card showing (ace-low) wins, and the call wins ties.

The winner takes all of the cards that are showing (n*players) and shuffles them and puts them on the bottom of their deck.

It rarely happens, but if there is a tie among non-callers, they will split the showing cards evenly.

Players must call some card when they flip the last card in their deck.

## Ace exception

If the called card is a king, if another player has an odd number (one or three) aces showing, the first one to say so wins the round.  Thus kings are not a guaranteed win.

## Winning

A player is out when they lose a round with all of their cards showing (i.e. they are out of cards).

The last player with a deck wins.
